Reference — Citizen AI Systems

Citizen Behaviors

Citizens are autonomous agents that pursue enabled tasks based on priority, proximity, and station need. Each behavior follows a state machine — a sequence of phases from searching for work to completing it. Understanding these cycles is key to efficient colony management.

🔍
Search
Prospecting — find asteroids and mark resource deposits
Skill: Prospecting

The Search behavior sends citizens on prospecting flights through the system to discover asteroids. Citizens scan their surroundings for uncharted asteroid bodies, mark them for future mining, and identify what resources they contain. This is the foundational behavior that feeds the entire resource pipeline — without Search, miners have nothing to target.

Phase 1
Searching
Fly outward from station, scan for asteroids
Phase 2
Approaching
Navigate toward detected body
Phase 3
Scanning
Identify resource composition
Phase 4
Marking
Flag asteroid for miners
Skill Benefit (Prospecting)
Higher Prospecting skill increases detection range and scan speed. Lv.1 scouts must fly close; Lv.10 scouts can detect asteroids at extreme range and identify yields faster.
Priority Behavior
Search is lowest priority — citizens with other enabled tasks (Mine, Build, Collect) will only Search when no higher-priority work is available. Ideal for citizens with no other enabled tasks.
Station Upkeep Role
Search feeds your mining pipeline. Early game: enable Search on 1–2 dedicated scouts while others Mine and Build. Mid game: as asteroid fields become sparse, searchers find distant deposits. Without active searchers, your miners will idle once nearby asteroids are depleted — the colony starves for raw materials.
⛏Feeds → Mine behavior 📥Feeds → Collect (marked resources) 🚀Needs → Docking Bay (return to refuel)
📥
Collect
Logistics — gather floating resources and deliver to storage
Skill: Logistics

Collect is the logistics backbone of your colony. Citizens fly to floating resource objects in orbit, pick them up, and deliver them to the nearest station storage that has capacity and accepts that resource type (respecting storage filters). Resources come from mining (ore chunks), decompression ejections, deconstruction refunds, and newly spawned resource fields.

Phase 1
Scanning
Find nearest collectible resource
Phase 2
Approaching
Fly to resource location
Phase 3
Picking Up
Load resource into cargo
Phase 4
Delivering
Fly to station, deposit in storage
Resource Filters
Each citizen can have per-citizen resource filters restricting which floating resource types they will pick up. Shown as a numbered badge on the Collect button. Set via the filter popup in the crew or citizen panel.
Skill Benefit (Logistics)
Higher Logistics skill improves pickup speed and effective detection range. Experienced collectors find resources faster and waste less time flying to distant objects. Lv.10 collectors are hyper-efficient.
Station Upkeep Role
Collect is critical for keeping resources flowing into storage. After miners crack asteroids, ore chunks float in orbit — collectors bring them in. After decompression events, collectors recover ejected materials. Without collectors, mined ore drifts forever. Tip: Use per-citizen resource filters to create specialized collector teams — one citizen collects only rare ores, another handles common materials.
▣Fills → Station Storage Bays ⚒Feeds → Foundry input regions 🔍Requires → Search (to mark resources)
🔨
Build
Construction — deliver materials and construct station modules
Skill: Construction

The Build behavior handles the entire construction pipeline: finding planned modules on the construction frontier, gathering the required resources from storage or orbit, delivering them to the blueprint, and then performing the construction work itself. Build-enabled citizens also perform deconstruction on modules marked for demolishing.

Phase 1
SearchingForWork
Find a frontier blueprint or deconstruction target
Phase 2
GatheringResources
Collect required materials from storage / orbit
Phase 3
Delivering
Fly to module, deposit materials
Phase 4
Constructing
Perform work until complete
Construction Frontier
Only Planned modules adjacent to at least one Operational module can receive resources. Builders automatically find the nearest frontier target. Targets are sorted by distance from core (closest first) to ensure orderly expansion.
Deconstruction
When modules are marked for demolishing (shift-click), Build-enabled citizens will also perform deconstruction work. This takes 40% of original build time. Refunds 25–65% of materials to orbit on completion.
Skill Benefit (Construction)
Higher Construction skill increases work rate — Lv.10 builders complete modules dramatically faster than Lv.1. Resource delivery speed is also improved.
Cancel Orders
The "Cancel Orders" button in station management clears all construction targets for workers on that station, transitioning them to SearchingForWork. Useful when priorities change mid-build.
Station Upkeep Role
Build is essential whenever you're expanding or modifying your station. Early game: enable Build on 2–3 citizens to rapidly expand from core. Mid game: keep 1–2 dedicated builders with high Construction skill for ongoing expansion. Build-enabled citizens will also automatically handle deconstruction requests. Tip: Construction is a high-priority task — citizens will prefer building over mining or searching when frontier targets exist.
▣Creates → Station Modules (all types) 🗑Handles → Deconstruction tasks 📥Requires → Collected resources in storage
⛏
Mine
Mining — extract raw ore from asteroids
Skill: Mining

Mining is the raw material source for everything in your colony. Citizens fly to known asteroids, orbit them, and extract ore chunks. Mined resources are ejected as floating objects that collectors must gather. Each asteroid has a composition determined by spawn probabilities — Iron (25%), Copper (20%), and Carbon (18%) are most common, while Uranium (3%) and Rare Earth (2%) are exotic.

Phase 1
Seeking
Find a known asteroid to mine
Phase 2
Approaching
Navigate to asteroid
Phase 3
Mining
Extract ore chunks over time
Phase 4
Finished
Asteroid depleted or cargo full → repeat
Ore Ejection
Mined ore chunks are ejected from the asteroid as floating Resource objects. They drift in orbit near the asteroid until a Collect-enabled citizen picks them up. Resource type is determined by asteroid composition.
Skill Benefit (Mining)
Higher Mining skill increases extraction rate. A Lv.10 miner extracts ore dramatically faster and can deplete asteroids in a fraction of the time. Critical for maintaining resource throughput in late game.
Station Upkeep Role
Mining is the entry point of the production chain. Ore → Ingots → Alloys → Components — it all starts with miners. Tip: Mining and Collect work as a pair. A miner creates ore chunks; a collector brings them to storage. Without collectors, mined ore floats uselessly. Without miners, collectors have nothing to gather. Enable both on different citizens for best throughput. Dedicate your highest-Mining-skill citizen to exclusively mine for maximum efficiency.
💎Produces → Raw Ore (T0 resources) 📥Requires → Collect (to bring ore to station) 🔍Requires → Search (to find asteroids)
🌾
Harvest
Agriculture — collect food from greenhouse modules
Skill: Agriculture

Harvest-enabled citizens collect food from Greenhouse modules on the station. Greenhouses passively accumulate food production over time (base rate 5.0 scaled by efficiency). If a greenhouse has a directed output (output direction set), food deposits directly into the adjacent enclosed storage region — no harvester needed. Harvesters are the legacy path for undirected greenhouses: they collect from the module's internal buffer.

Phase 1
Searching
Find a greenhouse with unharvested food
Phase 2
Approaching
Fly to the greenhouse module
Phase 3
Harvesting
Collect accumulated food (up to 64 buffer)
Phase 4
Delivering
Deposit food in station storage
Directed vs. Undirected Greenhouses
Directed: Food auto-deposits into adjacent storage region. No harvester needed. Buffer only holds overflow when region is full. Undirected: Food accumulates in module buffer (max 64). Harvester must collect it. Production halts when buffer is full.
Skill Benefit (Agriculture)
Higher Agriculture skill increases harvest speed — faster collection from module buffers. Less impactful for directed greenhouses since food auto-deposits, but still matters for the legacy harvester path.
Station Upkeep Role
Food keeps your colony alive. Citizens consume food during rest to replenish hunger. At 0% hunger, citizens starve and lose health each tick. Best practice: Set all greenhouses to directed output — food automatically fills the adjacent storage bay. This eliminates the need for dedicated harvesters entirely, freeing citizens for more valuable tasks. Only enable Harvest if you have undirected greenhouses or need emergency food collection.
🌱Collects from → Greenhouse modules 💤Feeds → Rest (citizens eat food during rest) 🏠Requires → Crew Quarters (population cap)
⚒
Smelt
Metallurgy — operate foundries to process ore into ingots and alloys
Skill: Metallurgy

Smelt-enabled citizens operate Foundry, Assembler, and Component Lab modules. The worker picks up input materials from the input storage region, loads them into the foundry, performs the smelting/crafting work, and deposits finished products into the output region. Foundries auto-detect recipes from input ore; Assemblers and Component Labs require manually assigned recipes.

Phase 1
SearchingForWork
Find a foundry with assigned recipe & available input
Phase 2
FetchingInput
Collect materials from input storage region
Phase 3
LoadingFoundry
Deliver materials to the foundry module
Phase 4
Smelting
Perform work (duration = recipe base_time)
Phase 5
Depositing
Place output in output storage region
Foundry Modes
Process Exact: Craft N items then stop. Accumulate: Produce until stockpile reaches target; auto-pause/resume. Indefinite: Never stop until input runs out or manually cancelled.
Membrane Model
The foundry sits in a wall between two storage regions. Its output direction determines which side receives products. All other adjacent enclosed cells are input sources. Input and output regions must both be enclosed for the foundry to function.
Auto-Detection (Foundry only)
Basic Foundries scan the input region for ore and automatically select the matching smelting recipe. No manual assignment needed. Workers just find ore and start smelting. Assemblers and Component Labs require manual recipe assignment.
Skill Benefit (Metallurgy)
Higher Metallurgy skill increases smelting speed — faster completion of each crafting job. At Lv.10, a metallurgist processes recipes significantly faster, dramatically increasing throughput.
Station Upkeep Role
Smelt transforms raw ore into the ingots and alloys that everything else requires. Without smelters, you have piles of useless ore. Strategy: Enable Smelt on 1–2 citizens and keep them exclusive (disable other tasks). A dedicated metallurgist quickly levels the Metallurgy skill, becoming dramatically more efficient. Position foundries between filtered storage bays — ore-only input, ingot-only output — and let the worker cycle autonomously.
⚒Operates → Foundry, Assembler, Component Lab 📥Requires → Collected ore in input bays 🔨Feeds → Build (ingots/alloys for construction)
🛡
Guard
Formation — battle stations, defensive positioning and combat
No Associated Skill

Guard assigns citizens to battle stations — a combat formation around the station. Unlike other behaviors, Guard is an exclusive WorkAssignment: enabling it overrides all other tasks. The citizen stops whatever they're doing and moves to their assigned formation position. From there, they engage hostile targets that enter range.

Phase 1
MovingToPost
Fly to assigned formation position
Phase 2
OnStation
Hold position, scan for threats
Phase 3
Engaging
Combat with hostile target
Phase 4
Returning
Return to post after engagement
Exclusive Assignment
Guard is different from other tasks — it's a WorkAssignment::BattleStations override. The citizen cannot mine, build, or smelt while on guard duty. They are fully committed to defense until you disable Guard.
Combat States
Citizens in combat cycle through states: Engaging (attacking target), Critical (HP below 30%), Dead. Critical citizens need immediate rest and medical attention. Dead citizens... don't recover.
Station Upkeep Role
Guard is a pure defense behavior — it produces nothing and consumes a citizen's entire productivity. Only enable it when threats are detected. Every citizen on guard duty is one fewer miner, builder, or smelter. Strategy: Keep Guard disabled during peacetime. When hostiles appear, enable it on 2–3 citizens (preferably those with lower industrial skill levels so you don't lose your best workers). Disable it immediately after the threat passes.
⚔Protects → Station from hostile entities 🛡Enhanced by → Shield Generator modules 🏥Recovery → Medical Bay (after combat wounds)
💤
Rest
Recovery — dock at port, regenerate energy, eat food
No Associated Skill

Rest sends citizens to their assigned docking port to recover. While docked, the citizen regenerates energy over time and consumes food from station storage to replenish hunger. Rest can be triggered manually (click the 💤 button) or happens automatically when a citizen's energy drops below critical threshold. Citizens also socialize during rest — forming relationships, sharing meals, playing games, and telling stories.

Phase 1
Seeking Port
Find assigned docking port or auto-assign one
Phase 2
Approaching
Fly to docking bay module
Phase 3
Docking
Dock ship at port, begin recovery
Phase 4
Resting
Recover energy, eat food, socialize
Phase 5
Undocking
Energy restored → resume work assignment
Energy & Hunger Recovery
Energy regenerates steadily while docked. Hunger is restored by consuming Food (resource ID 50) from station storage — if no food is available, hunger does not recover and continues to deplete.
Docking Port Assignment
Citizens are auto-assigned to a docking port on first rest. Each Docking Bay holds ~4 citizens at 100% efficiency. Assignments persist across rest cycles. Manual reassignment is available in the crew panel. Destroyed ports invalidate assignments.
Social Activities
Resting citizens interact with each other — sharing meals, playing games, stargazing, telling stories, debating. These activities build relationships (friendships and rivalries) and generate narrative events via the storytelling system.
Auto-Rest Triggers
Citizens auto-seek rest when energy drops below ~15% ("Exhausted"). At 0% energy, citizens cannot work effectively. Citizens with 0% hunger ("Starving") lose health each tick and desperately need food during rest.
Station Upkeep Role
Rest is the maintenance cycle that keeps your citizens alive and productive. The entire station exists to support this loop: Greenhouses produce food → Storage holds it → Docking Bays provide rest berths → Citizens eat and recover → Citizens return to work. Critical infrastructure: ensure you have enough Docking Bays (1 per 3–4 citizens), enough Greenhouses (directed output for auto-deposit), and enough Crew Quarters (12 pop cap each). Monitor the crew alert banner — "Exhausted" and "Starving" badges mean your infrastructure is falling behind your population.
The Death Spiral
If food production falls behind consumption: citizens starve → health drops → citizens die → fewer workers → less food production → more starvation. Break the spiral by: (1) building more Greenhouses with directed output, (2) temporarily sending all citizens to rest to eat remaining food, (3) reducing population pressure by decommissioning non-essential citizens.
🚀Requires → Docking Bay (berth for rest) 🌱Requires → Greenhouse (food production) 🏠Requires → Crew Quarters (population cap) 🏥Enhanced by → Medical Bay (+30% health recovery) 🎮Enhanced by → Recreation (+20% morale)

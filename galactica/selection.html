Selection System

How to click, drag, and wrangle every type of object in the game. The selection system is smart — it knows what you probably meant to click, and it handles the edge cases so you don't have to think about them.

◎
The Basics
Click, drag, and deselect
Selection in Stella Nova works like you'd expect from any strategy game, with a few tricks up its sleeve. Left-click to select, drag to box-select, and click empty space to deselect. The camera automatically follows whatever you select.
🖱️ Left Click
Click any entity to select it. The camera smoothly pans to follow it. If multiple things overlap, the game picks the most important one — your ships always take priority over background objects.
▭ Box Select
Click and drag to draw a selection rectangle. Everything of the same type inside the box gets selected. When mixed types are in the box, the game picks one category — usually the most useful.
⊕ Add to Selection
Hold ⌘ Cmd or Ctrl while clicking to add an entity to your current selection. Click an already-selected entity with modifier held to remove just that one.
✕ Deselect
Click empty space to deselect everything. Or press R to clear your selection instantly. Holding the modifier key while clicking empty space preserves your existing selection.

Multi-Selection
Box dragging, modifier keys, and category locking
You can select multiple entities of the same type at once. The game prevents confusing mixed selections — you can't accidentally select ships and asteroids in the same group.
📦 Box Select
Drag a rectangle. If the box captures ships and asteroids, only the highest-priority type is selected. Ships win in a box over most things — but planets actually have the highest box priority, since dragging over a solar system should grab planets, not the hundred ships orbiting them.
⌘ Modifier + Click
Holding the modifier and clicking adds individual entities one by one. Click a selected entity again to remove just that one. Only works within the same category — you can't modifier-click a planet into a group of ships.
⌘ Modifier + Box
Holding modifier while box-selecting adds the new group to your existing selection — as long as the categories match. Great for selecting scattered ships across different orbits.
🔒 Category Lock
Your selection is always a single type. Clicking a different type replaces everything, even with the modifier held. This keeps your selection clean and predictable.
💡
Empty space is safe: Clicking or box-selecting empty space with the modifier key held does nothing — your current selection is preserved. Without modifier, it clears everything.
🧠
Smart Behaviors
The system adapts to how you're playing
The selection system adjusts to your zoom level, resolves overlapping entities intelligently, and uses different priority rules for clicking vs. box-selecting.
🔍 Zoom-Adaptive Modules
When you're zoomed out, clicking a station selects the whole thing. Zoom in past a threshold and individual modules become clickable — each one highlighted separately. There's a comfortable buffer zone between the two modes so you won't flicker back and forth while scrolling.
🎯 Click vs. Box Priority
Clicking and box-selecting use different priority orders. Clicking favors ships (you usually want to click a specific ship). Box-selecting favors planets (dragging a big box over a system should grab planets, not tiny ships). The game picks the right behavior automatically.
📍 Overlap Resolution
When a ship is sitting on a planet, clicking hits the ship — even though the planet is bigger. The system checks all overlapping entities, sorts by priority, then picks the closest one at the best priority level.
📸 Camera Auto-Follow
Selecting any entity smoothly animates the camera to track it. For orbiting objects, the camera follows the entity's actual position — so it doesn't land on empty space where the planet was a moment ago.
🔍
Station module zoom thresholds: Modules appear when you zoom in fairly close, and they don't disappear until you zoom out much further. This asymmetry is intentional — once you're working with modules, slight zoom adjustments won't yank them away from you.

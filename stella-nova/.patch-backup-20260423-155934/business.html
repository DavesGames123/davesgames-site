Pricing Strategy Detail

The "Never Discount" Playbook

  • EA launch at $24.99: Below the $30 psychological barrier. Signals value-in-progress. Avoids KSP2's fatal $50 mistake. TESTED
  • Price increases during EA: As major features ship (mod support, multiplayer, expanded narrative), price steps up: $24.99 → $27.99 → $29.99 → $34.99 at 1.0. Early buyers are rewarded; later buyers pay for more value.
  • 10% maximum discount: RimWorld rarely exceeds 10% off. Factorio has never discounted. Frequent deep sales train customers to wait and signal declining value. Stella Nova caps at 10% during Steam seasonal sales.
  • DLC priced as expansions: $19.99–$29.99 for expansion-grade content. New systems, new mechanics, new narrative arcs. Not cosmetic packs. The Factorio Space Age model: $35 DLC sold 1.24M copies.
  • No bundles below cost: Base + DLC bundles at modest discount only (5–10%). Bundle pricing never drops below individual EA price.

Exit / Long-Term Scenarios

This Is a 10-Year Asset, Not a Quarterly Revenue Target

  • Scenario A — Lifestyle business: Conservative case. $2–5M over 5 years. Solo dev with near-zero burn = highly profitable at any scale. Papers, Please generated $41M over 10 years at $9.99.
  • Scenario B — Growth studio: Base case. $10–15M gross. Hire 3–5 devs to accelerate content. Modding ecosystem extends game life. DLC revenue compounds. RimWorld model: 7 developers, 5 DLCs, $116M+.
  • Scenario C — Breakout franchise: Optimistic case. $50M+ gross. Community-driven virality. Sequels, spin-offs, licensing. Factorio model: never sold, never discounted, compounding for 12 years.
  • What we won't do: Sell IP to a publisher. Implement MTX. Rush to 1.0. Abandon early access. The failure cases (KSP2, Spacebase DF-9) are cautionary tales, not templates.
Bottom Line

Stella Nova is capital-efficient by design. Near-zero burn rate. Premium pricing with proven comparables. Breakeven at 400 units. The upside range — from $2.4M to $50M+ over 5 years — is entirely driven by product quality and community engagement, not capital deployment. This is a game that makes money the moment it ships, and compounds for years through the long tail. The model works because the genre demands depth, and depth takes time — but once built, it generates revenue indefinitely.

Citizen Behaviors

Your citizens get on with whatever work you let them do. Tell one they can mine and collect, and they'll find ore and bring it home. Each citizen has six skills they level through practice, and they look after themselves: when tired or hungry, they fly home to eat and rest.

This page covers what each behavior does, when to turn it on, and how the pieces fit together.

· · ·
🔎
Search
Prospecting
levels the Prospecting skill

Searchers find hidden deposits and reveal them.

Resources don't start visible. A Prospecting citizen surveys an area and uncovers what's there. Once revealed, anyone can collect or mine it.

Search has the lowest priority of any job. A citizen with collect, mine, or build enabled will do those first. Search only runs when nothing else needs doing.

Stays close to home

Searchers only survey their stationed planet. They won't trespass on enemy space.

Why hidden in the first place

Discovery pacing. The colony explores outward over time instead of starting fully mapped.

How to play it

Give one or two citizens only Search. Without scouts, collectors eventually run out of targets and the colony stalls.

Collect
Logistics
levels the Logistics skill

Collectors haul floating resources home to station storage.

This is the workhorse. Anything drifting in orbit needs a collector. Without them, stuff piles up in space and never gets used.

Each collector has a list of resource types they'll pick up. The list starts empty. You decide what each one hauls: rare ores, bulk materials, or whatever's closest.

They rescue too

Collectors automatically retrieve downed crew. Your hauling fleet doubles as search and rescue.

They tidy up

Idle collectors with no cargo move misplaced resources into their proper storage cells.

Priority materials

Flag specific types as priority. They'll pass closer resources to grab priority kinds first.

How to play it

Pair collectors with miners. One mines, one hauls. Letting a single citizen do both creates slow round-trips.

Construct
Construction
levels the Construction skill

Constructors find what needs building, gather materials, and build it.

Your expansion engine. Place a blueprint or draft a module, and the crew handles materials and construction. They pull from orbital deposits or station storage, whichever's closer.

Construct has the highest priority of any job. A citizen with it enabled will choose it over everything else. Disable on miners you want kept on rocks.

Build
Levels fastest

Construction skill gains 4× normal XP. A dedicated builder outpaces other crew within a session.

Built-in speed boost

Even at level zero, construction runs about twice as fast as comparable hauling.

Two destinations

Station modules are new rooms on the grid. Orbital blueprints are free-flying structures. Same crew, same skill.

How to play it

Dedicate two or three citizens to Construct during expansion. They level fast and visibly accelerate your buildouts.

Hazardous

Construction has an accident risk. Keep collectors around for rescues.

Mine
Mining
levels the Mining skill

Miners chip ore from asteroids and dump it into orbit for collectors.

Your raw material spigot. Every ingot, alloy, and component starts as ore. Each asteroid has its own composition: one rich in iron, another with copper and carbon, another with something rare.

Miners don't haul. They extract, dump the chunk nearby, and return to the rock. Hauling is a collector's job. Each citizen specializes.

Strength in numbers

Up to four miners on one rock work faster together than four miners spread across four rocks. Cluster on rich veins.

First in, claim it

The first miner to touch an unclaimed asteroid locks it to their faction. Enemies can't mine claimed rocks.

Priority asteroids

Flag rocks as priority and your miners will pass closer ones to reach them.

How to play it

One or two dedicated miners, paired with collectors. Cluster on rich asteroids instead of spreading thin.

Hazardous

EVA work has an accident risk. Serious injuries put miners in the downed state. Keep haulers alive for rescue.

Harvest
Agriculture
levels the Agriculture skill

Harvesters pull food from greenhouses and stash it in storage.

Greenhouses grow food on their own. Whether you need harvesters depends on setup. A greenhouse with its output pointing into adjacent storage auto-deposits, no harvester required.

Harvest is the fallback. A harvester docks at the greenhouse, pulls food from the buffer, then carries it to filtered storage cells. Everything happens inside the station.

Direct your greenhouses

Point each greenhouse output at adjacent storage. Food flows in automatically and you may never need a harvester.

Food rots

Full buffers with nowhere to deposit eventually spoil. Direct the output or assign a harvester.

Easier on the body

Harvesters drain energy slowly. Long shifts are fine.

How to play it

Directed output for every greenhouse you can. Reserve Harvest for the cases that can't be directed.

Hazardous

Even agricultural work has accident risk.

Metallurgy
Smelting · Crafting · Fuel
levels the Metallurgy skill

Metallurgists work the foundries, turning ore into ingots, fuel, and parts.

Each production module sits between two storage areas. Input on one side, output on the other. A metallurgist docks, runs recipes until inputs run dry or output fills, then undocks.

Foundries and fuel processors auto-pick the recipe from whatever ore you stock. Assemblers and component labs need a recipe assigned manually.

Two kinds of modules

Foundries and fuel processors are smart. Toss in ore, they pick the recipe. Assemblers and component labs need explicit assignments.

Membrane model

Each module has one output direction. That side is output, everything else adjacent is input. Plan storage rooms around it.

Easier on the body

Indoor work. Long production runs are fine.

How to play it

Position foundries between filtered storage rooms: ore-only in, ingots-only out. The cycle runs hands-off.

Hazardous

Furnaces are dangerous despite being indoors. Accidents still happen.

💤
Rest
Recovery · Eating · Socializing
automatic, no toggle, no skill

When a citizen gets tired, hungry, or hurt, they fly home and recover.

Rest is automatic. The game manages it. Each citizen monitors energy, hunger, and health, and heads to a docking bay when something drops too low.

While docked they eat, regenerate, and socialize. When recovered, they return to their prior job. This is the maintenance loop that keeps the colony alive.

🛠
Back to work
Three reasons

Low energy, critical hunger, or serious injury. The first two show on the crew panel. Injuries appear as a health warning.

Push them harder

Flag a citizen to push through fatigue when a deadline matters. They'll work to nearly empty before stopping.

Jobs tire differently

EVA work (mining, building, hauling, scouting) drains energy 4× faster than indoor work. Harvesters and metallurgists can run long shifts.

Where the colony comes alive

Docked citizens chat, eat, play, gossip. Friendships, rivalries, romances. Stories emerge from these moments.

How to play it

Enough docking bays for the crew. Food always in storage. Watch for Exhausted or Starving badges on the crew panel.

Downed and rescue

Severe accidents drop citizens below the health threshold. They go downed, drifting in orbit, work paused. Collectors auto-rescue. No rescue in time, they die.

Death spiral

Food shortage means hungry citizens refuse work, which means less food production, which means more shortage. The cycle accelerates. Catch it before it spins up.

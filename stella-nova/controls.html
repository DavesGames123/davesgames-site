Getting Around
Pan, zoom, and track your fleet
You have two ways to move the camera: WASD keys for quick panning, or right-click drag for precise repositioning. Both detach the camera from whatever you're tracking — click on something again to snap back and follow it through its orbit.
WASD
hold
Keyboard Pan
Pan the camera in any direction. The further you're zoomed out, the faster you'll move — so panning feels consistent at every scale.
Right Click+Drag
drag
Mouse Pan
Hold right mouse button and drag to reposition the view. Great for small adjustments when you're zoomed in on a station.
Scroll ⟳
scroll
Zoom
Scroll to zoom in and out. The zoom centers on your cursor position — point at something distant and scroll to fly right to it.
QE
hold
Rotate Fleet Heading
Spin the directional heading for your ships. Q rotates counter-clockwise, E rotates clockwise. This sets the facing angle for formation commands.
📌
Auto-tracking: Whenever you click on a planet, ship, or station, the camera smoothly animates to it and follows its orbit. WASD or right-drag breaks the follow — click again to re-attach.
⚔
Commanding Your Fleet
Move ships, build structures, inspect stations
Select your ships, then use right-click to send them where you need them. The right mouse button is context-sensitive — it does different things depending on what you're pointing at.
Right Click
context
Move / Inspect
With ships selected, right-click open space to send them in formation. Right-click a station module to open its info popup. Right-click with a constructable active to cancel it.
B
toggle
Battle Stations
Toggle selected ships into combat mode. They'll hold position, face threats, and fight. Press B again to release them back to normal duties.
Left Click
build
Place Blueprint
When you've selected a constructable from the build menu, left-click anywhere to place it. It automatically enters orbit around the nearest planet.
R
press
Clear Selection
Deselect everything at once. You can also click empty space to deselect, but R is faster when your hands are on the keyboard.
⚔️
Formation auto-switch: When you right-click to move ships, any selected ship not already in Battle Stations is switched automatically. Your fleet knows when it's time to get serious.

Freeze the entire simulation. Orbits, construction, smelting, combat — everything stops. Hit Space again to resume. Great for setting up complex build orders.

Five speed levels from crawl to warp. Low speeds for precision like station building; high speeds to fast-forward through smelting and construction.

Pro Tips
Tricks that aren't obvious at first
🎯
Zoom to cursor: Scrolling zooms toward wherever your mouse is pointing. To quickly jump to a distant object, point at it and scroll in — you'll fly right to it without needing to pan.
🔍
Station module selection: Zoom in close to a station and individual modules become clickable. Zoomed out, the whole station selects as one unit. There's a comfortable buffer zone so you won't accidentally switch modes while scrolling.
⌘
Additive selection: Hold ⌘ Cmd (Mac) or Ctrl (Windows) while clicking or box-dragging to add to your selection instead of replacing it. Click an already-selected entity with modifier held to deselect just that one.
📦
Box select priority: When you drag a box over mixed entity types, the game picks the most important category automatically — ships take priority over asteroids, planets over everything. You'll always get the selection you expect.
🖱️
Right-click is contextual: It does the most useful thing based on what's under the cursor. Module? Info popup. Open space with ships selected? Formation move. No selection? Just pans the camera.
💥
Secret debug tool: Hold E and left-click anywhere to spawn a test explosion. It drifts naturally with orbital velocity. Fun for stress-testing your defenses (or just blowing things up).

How It Works
Newton's Law of Gravitation: F = G · m₁ · m₂ / r² Each body pulls every other body toward it. The force is proportional to both masses and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

Laplace Resonance (1:2:4): Three moons orbit with periods in exact integer ratios. Every time the inner moon completes 4 orbits, the middle completes 2, and the outer completes 1. Their gravitational nudges at conjunction keep them locked in sync.

Kepler's Laws
1st: Orbits are ellipses with the central mass at one focus.

2nd: A line from the body to the star sweeps equal areas in equal times — bodies speed up near periapsis.

3rd: T² ∝ r³ — farther orbits take disproportionately longer. For a 1:2:4 period resonance, radii are 1 : 1.59 : 2.52.

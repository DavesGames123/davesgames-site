HOW TO READ THE WHEEL▾
Dominants fall a fifth. Every X7 points at the chord five below it: G7 → C, E7 → Am. Tension, then home.
Diminished rises a half step. B° leans up into C. And because a dim7 is perfectly symmetric, it can rise from any of its four notes — those are the long lines crossing the center.
Any chord can be tonicized. Tap a triad to reveal its secondary dominants — the borrowed V7s that pull toward each of its neighbors.
